## 4. Cross-market comparison

- **Does NQ strength replicate on MNQ?** Cannot be tested — **no MNQ data**. (MNQ is
  the micro of NQ and would be the most direct robustness check; its absence is the
  key gap.)
- **Does it transfer to ES / MES?** Cannot be tested — **no ES or MES data**.
- **Is the edge broad, NQ-only, micro-only, or data-limited?** **Data-limited.**
  With exactly one market available, there is no independent market to confirm or
  refute the NQ edge. This is *not* evidence the edge is NQ-only — it is the absence
  of evidence either way.

---

## 5. Verdict — **DATA_LIMITED_INCONCLUSIVE**

Only **1** market (NQ) has clean local data; MNQ, ES, and MES are DATA_FAIL. A fair
multi-market robustness read **requires at least two independent markets** and is
therefore **not possible** here.

- **Not ROBUST_SUPPORT:** requires the frozen logic to be positive and not
  top-winner-dependent across *multiple independent* markets — only one is available.
- **Not NQ_ONLY_SUPPORT:** that label requires ES/MES/MNQ to be *run and fail to
  confirm*. They were never runnable, so we cannot say the edge is NQ-only.
- **Not WEAK_OR_NO_SUPPORT:** nothing failed; the available NQ window is unchanged
  and still positive.

The honest classification is **DATA_LIMITED_INCONCLUSIVE**: the robustness question
is unanswered for lack of clean local cross-market data.

---

## 6. Interpretation & recommendation

- **Diagnostic only** — nothing here is tuned, and the multi-market test did not
  run for three of four symbols. The single-market NQ result is re-confirmed, not
  improved.
- **No paper/live recommendation. No promotion.** S26 remains not deployable
  (CONCENTRATED_SUPPORT at D8, inconclusive D6/D7).
- **The robustness ladder rung is unresolved, not passed.** The CONCENTRATED_SUPPORT
  concern from D8 (edge tied to a few strong NQ calendar windows) is *exactly* what a
  cross-market test would stress — and that test could not be run.

**Recommendation for S26-D10:** the binding blocker is **data availability**, not a
new test idea. The right next step is a **pre-registered data-provisioning + fair
multi-market protocol** (mirroring the D4-before-D5 discipline):
  1. Decide *which* markets and which clean local daily files would constitute a fair
     test (MNQ as the direct NQ-micro check; ES/MES as a correlated-but-distinct
     instrument), and the symbol→loader convention they must follow.
  2. Pre-register the robustness PASS/FAIL gate **before** any cross-market data is
     run or viewed.
  3. Only then place clean offline CSVs in `data_offline/` and run once.
If clean cross-market data cannot be sourced offline, S26 robustness stays
**INCONCLUSIVE** and the branch should be held — **not** promoted on the
single-market NQ evidence alone. Do not deploy.
